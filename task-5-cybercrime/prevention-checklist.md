**Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (For College Students in India)**



**Personal \& Account Safety**



1. Use strong, unique passwords for each account. Avoid using your name, birthday, or simple passwords like 123456.
2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps for extra security.
3. Do not click unknown links sent via WhatsApp, SMS, Instagram, or email, especially links related to prizes, jobs, or bank updates.
4. Never share OTP, passwords, or personal details with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank or official organization.
5. Log out from public computers in libraries, labs, or cyber cafés after using your accounts.
6. Keep your phone and laptop updated with the latest security updates and antivirus software.



**Financial / UPI Safety (Important)**



7. Remember: Entering a UPI PIN is only for sending money, not receiving money. If someone asks you to enter a PIN to receive money, it is a scam.
8. Do not approve unknown “Collect Requests” on Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, or any UPI app.
9. Never install screen-sharing apps (like AnyDesk or TeamViewer) if someone asks you to for bank or payment issues.



**Social Media Safety**



10. Keep your social media accounts private and avoid sharing personal information like address, phone number, college ID, etc.
11. Do not accept friend requests from unknown people, as fake accounts are often used for scams and phishing.



**Reporting Cybercrime in India**



12. If you become a victim of cybercrime, immediately report it to:
* National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
* Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
