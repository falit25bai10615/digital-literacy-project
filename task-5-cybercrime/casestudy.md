One of the most common cybercrimes of the present day is UPI and online payment fraud. This type of fraud is more common in countries like India, where digital payments are highly used. In this type of cybercrime, the fraudster lures the victim into paying money or disclosing their banking-related confidential information such as OTP, UPI PIN, or credit/debit card details.



The steps that are generally followed by fraudsters while committing a fraud related to UPI are as follows: First, the fraudster contacts the victim by phone call or message by impersonating a bank employee, delivery agent, customer care executive, or buyer/seller of an online marketplace. Then, the fraudster sends a "Collect Request" via a UPI application or a fake link to the victim, asking him/her to enter their UPI PIN to collect the payment. In reality, entering the PIN approves a payment, and money is deducted from the victim’s account. In some cases, fraudsters also ask for OTP or ask the victim to install screen-sharing apps to capture banking details.



The most common victims of this type of cybercrime are students, seniors, online traders, and those who are not too familiar with online payment systems. The impact of this type of cybercrime is that the victim loses money, suffers mental stress, loses faith in online payment systems, and may have to go through a lengthy process of resolving the issue through the bank.



This type of cybercrime proves that there is a certain amount of risk involved in online payment systems, and users should never share their OTP, UPI PIN, or authorize unknown payment transactions.
